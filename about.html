// why we started

A year ago, we were three kids who had never shipped anything real. No class taught the thing we actually wanted — how to build with AI, not just chat with it. So we taught ourselves: late nights, broken code, a hundred dead ends, until the night something we made actually ran.

That moment — your thing, working, in front of you — is the one that quietly rewrites what a kid thinks they're capable of. A few months later it put us on a global stage with a trophy over our heads.

And we kept thinking the same thing: imagine reaching this at twelve instead of sixteen. That's the entire reason Code Share Academy exists — to hand the next kid that moment, years earlier than we stumbled into it.

Good AI education shouldn't be priced for adults and invisible to twelve-year-olds. So we built the opposite.

// the window

Every kid is about to use AI. The few who learn to build with it are the ones who get noticed.

Most parents can feel it coming — AI is about to reshape the work our kids grow into. The good news is it cuts both ways: the kid who learns to build with it now is the one who stays ahead of it, not the one it replaces. That window won't stay open long.

// the obvious question

Why trust three high-schoolers with your kid? Fair. Here's the honest answer.

The usual pitch is a teacher who learned to code decades ago. We're the opposite — on purpose. We're a few steps ahead of your kid, in the exact tools they'll open, and we were the beginner last year, not last century. Kids lean in for someone who feels reachable: they build more, ask more, and quit less when the person teaching them was just in their seat. The research on near-peer teaching says the same thing we felt — the smaller the gap, the more a kid is willing to ask the question they're afraid is dumb. Youth isn't the caveat here. It's the method. And the work behind it isn't a hobby: it's a real AI product and a global first-place finish.

// what we believe

01

Build, don't just use.

Using AI is the floor — every kid will get there by accident. Building with it is the edge, and it's learnable, even at twelve.

02

Real code, real projects.

Every student ships something only they would make, and walks out able to explain every line of it. No toy worksheets.

03

Judgment over hype.

We teach where AI is wrong, where it's risky, and when not to trust it. The goal is a kid who's powerful with it and skeptical of it — not dazzled.

04

Close, not distant.

Near-peer teachers who were beginners a year ago. We speak the language your kid already speaks, and that's the whole point.

05

Small on purpose.

Twelve seats a cohort. We'd rather go deep with a few and watch them finish than go wide with a crowd and lose half.

06

Mission over margin.

First class free, then $25 only when they show up. We're here to make the next builders — not to sell you a course you'll forget you bought.
